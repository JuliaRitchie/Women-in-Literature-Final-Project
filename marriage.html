Quotes About Marriage

"Silly Novels by Lady Novelists" — George Eliot

"For all this, she as often as not marries the wrong person to begin with..."

"The Lifted Veil" — George Eliot

"She was my wife, and we hated each other."

"The fluctuations of hope and fear, confined to this one channel, made eachday in her presence a delicious torment.

"To my Dear and Loving Husband" — Anne Bradstreet

"I prize thy love more than whole mines of gold, or all the riches the East doth hold."

"A Letter to Daphnis" — Anne Finch

"They err, who say that husbands can't be lovers."

"A Married State" — Katherine Philips

"Turn, turn apostate to love's levity."

"To the Ladies" — Lady Mary Chudleigh

"Wife and servant are the same, but only differ in the name: For when that fatal knot is tied, which nothing, nothing, can divide...shun, oh shun that wretched state..."

"A Receipt for Writing" — Mary Alcock

"They're married — and your history's over."

"A Room of One's Own" — Virginia Woolf

"Some collaboration has to take place in the mind between the woman and the man before the act of creation can be accomplished. Some marriage of opposites has to be consummated. The whole of the mind must lie wide open if we are to get the sense that the writer is communicating his experience with perfect fullness.

"Love and Friendship" — Jane Austen

"I felt that on him the happiness or misery of my future life must depend."
