Quotes About Friendship

"The Faith of a Writer" — Joyce Carol Oates

"We learned to imitate her."

"Don't cast sidelong glances and compare yourself to others among your peers."

"Give yourself up in admiration, even in the adoration, of another's art."

"Feminine Heroines" — Elaine Showalter

"The feminine novelists needed intimacy with other women both for inspiration and for sympathetic friendship, but they are much less likely than male novelists to have personal contact with other professional writers."

"Female novelists were compulsively drawn to compare themselves both to Brontë and Eliot..."

"Harriet Beecher Stowe claimed to have managed a two-hour conversation with Charlotte in a gossipy seance..."

"As Charlotte Brontë and George Eliot increasingly came to dominate their period and to represent the models against which other women were measured, they too became objects of both feminine adulation and resentment.

"The Life of Charlotte Brontë and Letters of Elizabeth Gaskell" — June Foley

"Gaskell in a sense exploited their association from the start."

Gaskell doubtless wrote Charlotte Brontë's (biography) out of love for her friend. But perhaps she also wrote it to tell the tale of her own..."

"Northanger Abbey" — Jane Austen

"...might have informed Catherine, had she been more expert in the development of other people's feelings, and less simply engrossed by her own."

"A Room of One's Own" — Virginia Woolf

"It is natural for the sexes to co-operate."

"Love and Friendship" — Jane Austen

"She received me with equal surprise though not with equal cordiality."
